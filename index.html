Background

About Me

I grew up in Peru with questions that never left me: Why do some places have clean water and others don't? Why do children in the same country face such different futures? Those questions led me to economics — and economics led me to data, computation, and public policy.

My work sits at the intersection of rigorous quantitative research and real institutional problems. I have designed surveys for 7,000-household randomized evaluations in Sierra Leone, built ETL pipelines for Peru's National Pension Office, automated analytical workflows for the IDB, and contributed to impact evaluations at J-PAL and the World Bank.

Now, at UChicago's Harris School, I am expanding my computational toolkit to match my research training — learning to deploy data science at scale in service of governance, accountability, and social equity. I am drawn to roles where analytical depth meets civic purpose.

CivicPulse Chicago

Where do civic complaints and public spending fail to align — and what does that tell us about service equity across Chicago's neighbourhoods?

An urban civic data project integrating Chicago 311 service requests, census demographics, and public budget allocations to surface patterns of institutional responsiveness and neighbourhood neglect. The analysis maps complaint density, per-capita service gaps, and budget mismatches across the city's 77 community areas — building toward a spatial civic sentiment score.

Python GeoPandas Plotly Census API Spatial Analysis Dashboard
