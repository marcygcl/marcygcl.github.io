About Me

Background

I grew up in Peru asking questions about how societies work — why some public systems function and others don't, why well-designed policies so often fail in practice, and what it actually takes to change institutional outcomes. Those questions led me to economics, and economics led me to nearly a decade of applied research across Latin America, West Africa, and the United States.

I have designed field evaluations for national-scale RCTs in Sierra Leone, built data pipelines for public institutions in Lima, automated analytical workflows for the IDB, and contributed to impact evaluations at J-PAL and the World Bank. Across those experiences, one thing became clear: the problems I cared most about were increasingly shaped by computational systems I didn't yet fully understand.

That's what brought me to the MSc in Computational Analysis and Public Policy at UChicago — not a career pivot, but a deepening. I wanted the technical fluency to work on the full problem. Today I work at the intersection of data science, public policy, and civic technology, with a particular interest in governance, service delivery, and institutional accountability.

CivicPulse Chicago

When residents file 311 complaints about broken lights, potholes, or failing infrastructure — does the city listen equally? And does the budget reflect what people actually need?

CivicPulse integrates Chicago's 311 service requests, census demographics, and public budget allocations to map where institutional responsiveness breaks down. The analysis surfaces complaint density, per-capita service gaps, and budget mismatches across the city's 77 community areas. The goal is a spatial civic sentiment score — a diagnostic tool for understanding which neighbourhoods bear the weight of unmet public need, and whether public spending follows or ignores that signal.

Field Data Systems — Sierra Leone RCT

I spent a year in Freetown working on an RCT across 450 communities. The research question was about vaccines and electrification. The real question was: how do you build rigorous infrastructure in fragile systems?

Programmed complex ODK survey instruments with skip patterns and enumerator safeguards, designed sampling frameworks for 7,000+ households, and built real-time supervision dashboards that 150 enumerators, 5 supervisors, and 2 PIs used for daily field decisions. The vaccination arm contributed to evidence of a 26 percentage-point increase in uptake through last-mile mobile deployment.

Probabilistic Record Linkage — IDB

Government databases in Jamaica and Suriname held millions of citizen records — in different formats, with different naming conventions, no shared ID. How do you link them without losing the people who fall through the cracks?

Built a Python fuzzy-matching and probabilistic record-linkage algorithm for the IDB, reducing false matches across fragmented ministry databases and supporting 20–30 analysts working on two national RCTs. Also automated 6+ Stata analytical pipelines covering data cleaning, balance tests, attrition checks, and heterogeneity analysis.
